Est. 2014

Born From a Passion for the Perfect Cup

Brewhaus began in a tiny 200 sq ft space in SoHo, New York. Our founder, Elena Voss, had spent three years traveling through Ethiopia, Colombia, and Indonesia — not as a tourist, but as a student of coffee. She slept on farms, learned to cup alongside veteran roasters, and fell in love with the precision and artistry behind every great bean.

When she returned to New York in 2014, she wanted to share that discovery with her city. Brewhaus opened its doors with just six drinks, two pastries, and an obsessive commitment to sourcing. Word spread fast. Within a year, we'd outgrown the original space twice.

Today, Brewhaus operates three locations across Manhattan, maintains direct-trade partnerships with 12 farming cooperatives, and roasts every batch in-house at our SoHo flagship. We haven't changed what we're about — we've only gotten better at it.

The Craft

Roasted With Intention

Our roasting lab is at the heart of the SoHo flagship. Head Roaster Daniel Park roasts every batch in small lots of 20lbs or less, allowing for precise temperature control and consistency that industrial roasters can't achieve.

Each origin is profiled differently — a light Ethiopian natural might be roasted to 202°C over 10 minutes to preserve its floral acidity, while a Sumatran wet-hulled bean gets a longer, lower-heat development to highlight its earthy, full-body character.

Coffee is roasted to order — never sitting on shelves for more than two weeks. When you taste Brewhaus, you're tasting freshness.
